About Our Charity

Annai Charitable Trust a non profit organisation, was started in the year 2007 by Mr.G.Saravanan

Our primary Purpose and objective and activities of the Trust. Our is a voluntary services motive no profit and non governmental organisation. It was framed by social workers deducted to the services of the deserving socio economically underprivileged communities like scheduled castes, scheduled tribes and Backward classes, most Backward classes. These people are away from the basic facilities like education, health, nutrition, food, Drinking water, economically independent and good shelter.


Our organisation decided to work within the financial limits like donations from the public and from the members for the development of the deserved families with this aspect. We have carried out a few modest activities in the area our organisation is SALEM RURAL DISTRICT and Salem Urban District in Tamil Nadu. We successfully implemented some of the programs as detailed below among our target people with the help of some local level. Well wishers of our organisation.

Our Achievemtns in Numbers

Annai Charitable Trust is dedicated to empowering poor people, medical relief & blind individuals through education, healthcare, and technology. We provide Braille literacy programs, vocational training, eye care services, and assistive devices to enhance the quality of life for the visually impaired. Our initiatives include job placement, mental health support, and community engagement activities to foster an inclusive society. By raising awareness and advocating for equal opportunities, we strive to create a world where blindness is not a barrier to success and fulfilment.

Health camp programme

To provide free medical assistance to the economically weaker section of the society this year we have conducted 2 medical camps in our area. The first camp was conducted in the of January 06.01.2014 and the second camp in the month of February (07.02.2014). women, Children, aged poor people in and around our area participated in the programmes around 625 patients were medically examined and treated. A team of six doctors, nine nurses, our staff and volunteers were engaged in these camps. The patients were supplied with medicines at free of cost. The severe cases which need further treatment were referred to the special doctors.

S.H.G. Formation and Training Expenses:

Women of economically weaker sections of the society were identified and motivated to form groups among them. The importance of savings and the value of group activity were insisted to them repeatedly. Their attitudes were changed toward saving. This year we have formed 20 Self-Help Groups in our area. Savings and Microcredit are the main activities of the sanga. The saved amount is rotated among the members as credit at a low rate of interest. The members save regularly once in a month and meet twice in a month. They also participate in the public and our trust functions.

Vocational Training Programme:

Skill graining was five to both adult girls and boys on wire knitting, soap and washing powder making, candle making, fruits jam and pickle making and tailoring, Embroidery, Spinning, Painting, Typewriting.
